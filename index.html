About
my PhD

My PhD thesis is about basaltic and phonolitic volcanism of Petite terre, a small island near the island of Mayotte (Comoros, Western Indian Ocean). Following the discovery of an active submarine volcano 50 km east of Mayotte in 2018 the idea that volcanic activity in Mayotte was not extinct grew stronger as evidence from magmatic CO2 degassing and existence of presumably young volcanism were already known. These evidences of active volcanism focus on the small island of Petite Terre located in the lagoon around Mayotte main island Grande Terre.

Petite Terre itself is composed by the volcanic products of the 4 maars (crater leaved by a specific type of volcanic explosive activity, think about Pompeii eruption) covering several scoria cones (formed by much less explosive activity with lava flows as Kilauea or Piton de la Fournaise). Though the explosive activity seems rather young it precise age remains unknown as well as it eruption style since the volcanism in the area is very poorly studied.

Through my PhD I'm working on deciphering the precise sequence of events during these eruptions. To do so, I use both volcanology, petrology and geochemistry to probe not only HOW the eruption happen but also WHY the eruption happen. These data, will be used later by other scientists to understand these very peculiar volcanoes and the authorities to create evacuation models in case of volcanic unrest in the area.
